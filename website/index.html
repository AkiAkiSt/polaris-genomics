Non-coding variant interpretation

POLARIS

Most disease-linked DNA changes lie in the genome's regulatory dark matter. POLARIS triangulates independent, fallible evidence into a calibrated guess at the causal variant, its mechanism, and the gene it acts on — and tells you how much to trust it.

These are hypotheses, not proven causation — a predicted molecular effect is not proof a variant causes disease. POLARIS shows its uncertainty and its disagreements on purpose.
Why this is hard

A signal in the neighborhood, not a name

Genome-wide studies flag regions associated with disease — but ~90% of the time the signal is non-coding, dozens of variants are inherited together, and no one tells you which variant is causal, what it does, or which gene it controls. Every clue we have is individually noisy.

1 · Which variant?

A locus contains many correlated variants. Statistical fine-mapping narrows it to a credible set with per-variant probabilities (PIP).

2 · What effect?

Does it break a transcription-factor grip site? Sit in a conserved enhancer? We score this transparently, from biophysics — not a black box.

3 · Which gene?

The target is often not the nearest gene. Colocalization and locus-to-gene models link the signal to an effector.

4 · How sure?

The powerful AI oracles disagree and are poorly calibrated. Credibility comes from convergence, with honest confidence.

Two principles, made mathematical

The guardrails that keep it honest

01

Functional ≠ pathogenic

A molecular effect is a hypothesis about mechanism — not proof of disease. POLARIS keeps them as separate multiplied factors, so a variant can be "functional" yet not disease-linked, and vice-versa.

02

Distrust any single oracle

Every evidence channel is a fallible witness. POLARIS learns how much to trust each one from the data (a latent-truth model), rather than hand-setting weights or believing one score.

surprising Rarity is inverted

For common-variant disease, causal variants are more common, not rarer — the opposite of rare inherited disorders. A tool that assumed "rarer = more dangerous" would be systematically wrong. Replicated in both diseases.

signal Conservation marks causal variants

Positions evolution has protected across mammals are more likely functional. Fine-mapped causal variants are significantly more conserved than their innocent LD neighbors.

Radical honesty

What POLARIS does not do

The honesty is the whole point. These outputs are hypotheses to prioritize experiments — not diagnoses.

It abstains on hard cases

It flags the distal FTO→IRX3 case and assigns its lowest confidence to the imprinted DUSP8 locus — its two known errors. Ranking by confidence and withholding the least-sure raises precision.

It shows disagreements

Nearest gene, L2G, and ground truth often disagree. We display all three rather than hiding behind one, and mark when the transparent molecular signal contradicts the proximal gene call.

It names its blind spots

No single molecular channel resolves the causal variant well. Real-data colocalization is weak because GTEx lacks pancreatic islets — the disease-relevant tissue. We report this, not around it.
